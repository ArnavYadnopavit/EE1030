Question:\\
Show that the points $\vec{A}$ \brak{-2\hat{i}+3\hat{j}+5\hat{k}}, $\vec{B}$ \brak{\hat{i}+2\hat{j}+3\hat{k}} and $\vec{C}$ \brak{7\hat{i}-\hat{k}} are collinear\\
\solution

From \tabref{tab:1}\\
The matrix
\begin{align}
\myvec{B-A & C-A}^\top=\myvec{3 & -1 & -2 \\ 9 & -3 & -6}\\
\xleftrightarrow{R_2 =R_2 -3R_1} 
\myvec{3 & -1 & -2 \\ 0 & 0 & 0}
\end{align}
$\therefore$ the rank=1 which implies points are collinear.
